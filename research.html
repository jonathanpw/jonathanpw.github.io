Multistate Markov and hidden Markov models


  • J P Williams, G H Hermansen, H M Nygård, G Clayton, S A Rustad, and H Strand (202x). Bayesian hidden Markov models for latent variable labeling assignments in conflict research: application to the role ceasefires play in conflict dynamics. In review.
    Link: Manuscript. Link: Time capsuled code (R code).

  • E B Kendall†, J P Williams, G H Hermansen, F Bois, and V H Thanh (202x). Beyond time-homogeneity for continuous-time multistate Markov models. In review.
    Link: Manuscript.
    Description: Extending the ideas of the paper below, this research establishes how to fit time-inhomogeneous, continuous-time HMM, not restricted to the assumption that transition rates are piecewise constant functions of time. The approach relies on numerical solutions to the Kolmogorove forward equations, and doesn't suffer from the biases that we demonstrate for the matrix-exponential solution applied (as is commonly done) when the transition rates are not piecewise constant.

  • J P Williams, C B Storlie, T M Therneau, C R Jack Jr, and J Hannig (2020). A Bayesian approach to multi-state hidden Markov models: application to dementia progression. Journal of the American Statistical Association 115 (529), pp.16-31.
    Link: Manuscript. Link: Supplementary material. Link: Time capsuled code (R code).
    Description: Primarily, this research determines how to fit a time-inhomogeneous, continuous-time HMM, based on the assumption that the transition rates are piecewise constant functions of time. The approach is exact, and relies on the matrix-exponential solution to the Kolmogorove forward equations.
